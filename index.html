Understanding HTML5

HTML5 is the latest version of the Hypertext Markup Language, used to structure webpages effectively.
HTML5 was officially released on October 28, 2014, by the World Wide Web Consortium (W3C).
The development of HTML5 was a collaborative effort involving various organizations and individuals,
including the W3C and the Web Hypertext Application Technology Working Group (WHATWG).
The goal was to improve the language to support the latest multimedia and graphical content on
the web while ensuring it remained easily readable by humans and consistently interpretable by computers and devices.

Why Use HTML5?

HTML5 introduces semantic elements, improved accessibility, and better SEO practices.
